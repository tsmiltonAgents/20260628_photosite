Hello, and welcome. My name is Tom Milton and I make photographs the old-fashioned way — on film, one frame at a time. I started carrying a battered Leica around in 1995 and never really stopped. Everything you see here was shot on emulsion, run through chemistry, and scanned in my kitchen.

This page is hand-coded, deliberately humble, and updated whenever a roll comes back from the lab. Pour a coffee, click around, and please say hello.

🔨 The Kit

I shoot a small, well-loved family of cameras — mostly a Leica M6, a Pentax 67 for the big landscapes, and a Hasselblad 500CM when I want square frames and slow mornings. Favourite stocks: Kodak Portra 400 for skin and light, Fuji Velvia 50 for green valleys, and Ilford HP5 for the black-and-white days.
