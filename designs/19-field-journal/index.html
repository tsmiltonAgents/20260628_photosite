Field Notes

— kept by Tom Milton
A travelling photographer’s journal · 1995–2020
Property of T. Milton · Return if found

Twenty-five years, a duffel of film, and one stubborn rule: shoot what stops you in the road. These pages are the bits I scribbled between frames — where I stood, what light I was chasing, and the strangers kind enough to look back. Mind the coffee stains.

1995 — 1997 · EARLY ROADS
✦

Spring ’95 — the Black Forest

Black Forest, Germany

Got properly lost on the first morning, which turned out to be the whole point. The light comes down through the pines in columns like something built rather than grown. Loaded the Rollei and just waited.

FILM LOG // Roll 03. Rolleiflex 2.8F. Portra 160, rated box speed. Overcast then a 20-minute window of sun. Metered for the shadows and prayed.

Autumn ’96 — Olympic NP

Olympic National Park, Washington, USA

Rangers call it a temperate rainforest. I called it a cathedral and shut up. Mounted the Hasselblad on the tripod, stood under the canopy for two hours, and only tripped the shutter four times. *worth it.

Late ’97 — Tuscan hills

Tuscany, Italy

Drove the Pentax 645 up into the hills above Siena. The whole valley is stitched together like a quilt somebody’s grandmother made — greens and golds, all seams and patches.

1998 — 2000 · CHASING LIGHT
→

Harvest ’98 — wheat country

Tuscany, Italy

Last good light of the day exploding through the heads of the wheat. The M6 doesn’t care that your hands are shaking — you just have to be quicker than the sun.

Summer ’99 — Glencoe

Glencoe, Scottish Highlands

Scotland gives you four seasons before lunch. Caught the river at the one moment the clouds opened. Velvia made the greens almost dishonest — but that’s how it felt, so I’ll allow it.

NOTE TO SELF // Velvia 50 has no patience for shadows. Bracket everything. Bring more socks. The midges are not a myth.
2001 — 2003 · QUIET PLACES
✦

Oct ’01 — the Cascades

Washington, USA

Up before the birds to beat the fog. Failed. The fog won, the fog always wins — so I photographed the fog instead. The Pentax 67 negatives are the size of a postcard. Worth the weight.

Spring ’03 — Banff & Norway

Banff, Canada · then north to Norway

Two cold mornings, two continents. The cabin at Banff at first light, then weeks later a pine forest in Norway so silent I could hear the film advance. Shot the Norway frames on HP5 — some places only want to be remembered in black and white.

2002 — 2005 · COASTS & LONG ROADS
→

Summer ’02 — Big Sur

Big Sur, California, USA

Pulled the Contax out at the cliff edge where the Pacific just stops pretending to be calm. You can taste the salt in the grain. *do not lean further.

’05 — over the Dolomites

Dolomites, Italy

Tried the Cinestill 800T on the mountain pass at dusk, headlights coming on, fog rolling over the road. Tungsten film makes the cold air go properly blue. The long way home is always the better photograph.

FILM LOG // Cinestill 800T, pushed 1 stop. Canon AE-1, 50mm. The red halation around the headlights — left it in. That’s the whole mood.
2006 — 2010 · FURTHER NORTH, FURTHER OUT
✦

Aug ’06 — Iceland

Somewhere off the Ring Road, Iceland

Followed the sound of water for half a mile and found a falls nobody had bothered to name. Velvia and a Nikon FM2 and a footbridge slick with spray.

’08 — Namibia

Namib Desert, Namibia

The dunes don’t hold still — the wind keeps redrawing them all day. Ektar loves that orange sand almost as much as I do. Climbed one at 5am to get the shadow line. Got sand in the Hasselblad. Worth it.

’10 — Lake District

Lake District, England

A foggy dock at the edge of a still lake. Cinestill 50D, slow and patient. Some mornings the whole world is just a pier and the white nothing past it.

2012 — 2020 · PEOPLE I MET ALONG THE WAY
→

Winter ’12 — Havana

Havana, Cuba

Number fourteen in a little series — strangers who said yes. He was leaning in a doorway off the Malecón, cigar gone cold, and he held the look for exactly one frame. Tri-X, available light, no second chance. *the best ones never need a second.

SERIES // “Strangers” — one roll of Tri-X, one frame each, ask first, no setups. No. 14: Havana. Leica M4, 35mm, f/2, 1/125. Thanked him in bad Spanish.

Spring ’20 — Paris

Café de Flore, Paris, France

Last entry, last roll before everything went quiet that year. A coffee at the Flore, Gold 200 in the little Olympus, the whole café going about its small warm business. A good place to leave the journal for now.
